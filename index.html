The Blind Spot

Your tools show you numbers.
They don't find the problems hiding in them.

Every contractor we talk to says they track their costs. And every one of them has a story about a project that went sideways and they didn't see it coming. Those two things can't both be true. Unless the tracking isn't working the way you think it is.

Your cost report shows three columns

Budget. Actual. Variance. That's it. It doesn't tell you why you're over. It doesn't separate a $500K scope change from a $500K overrun. Those are two completely different problems with two completely different fixes. And your report treats them the same.

Your PM reviews costs on Friday

The overrun started Tuesday. By Friday, it's been bleeding for four days. By the time it shows up in a monthly report, it's been bleeding for three weeks. A $30K problem became a $300K problem because nobody saw it in time.

Small creep is invisible in a spreadsheet

A 2% creep across 15 cost codes doesn't jump out on any single line item. But on a $15M project, that's $300K. No human reviewing a spreadsheet catches that. It only shows up when something reads every number on every project and connects the pattern.

Your system tells you what happened. Not what's coming

If concrete is trending 6% over at 40% complete, what does that mean at 100%? Your current tools can't answer that. Ours can. And we flag it while there's still time to course correct.

Showing numbers and finding problems are two different things.

Your current tools tell you what happened. We tell you what's happening right now, what's about to happen, and exactly what to do about it. With dollar amounts.
